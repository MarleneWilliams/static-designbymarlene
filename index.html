Hi, my name is Marlene Williams. I do front-end web development + build websites using Drupal.

What I do

I have worked with Drupal since 2007 and have built sites using versions 5, 6, and 7. I know how to install and configure Drupal to serve a wide variety of functions, from a simple blog to a small online store to a university site. Since I'm not a PHP or backend developer and generally don't write my own functionality, I have become proficient at selecting and using contributed modules.

My favorite website-building tasks, however, involve theming, which is writing and manipulating the code that gives a site its look and feel. An important aspect of that is responsive web design, making websites that respond well to the wide variety of modern screen sizes and environments. I enjoy building sites that look good now—on your desktop as well as your phone—and that will be compatible with future technologies.

My work style is detailed and analytical and my code is clean and well-structured. I love learning, so my work contstantly improves, and I enjoy coming up with creative and reusable solutions to problems.

Technologies I am familiar with and have used include HTML, CSS, jQuery/JavaScript, Drupal, WordPress, Git, Unix command line, Sass, Compass, Middleman, Gulp, Grunt, Angular JS, etc. I have a background in art and know Photoshop, Illustrator, and Corel Painter well.

I work well with people, on teams as well as directly with clients. As an independent contractor, I have experience taking a website from the planning stages, through development, and into training and support.

Currently I'm available for part-time contract work doing frontend development and Drupal site-building.

Community

Connecting with and contributing to Drupal and general tech communities keeps me inspired and on my toes. Here's a brief list of my involvements:

Devsigner
I am the founder and main organzier of the Devsigner conference, which features sessions and workshops focusing on front-end web design and development techniques, tips and tools. We also aim to inspire our technically inclined creative community with session topics that bridge the gap between art and code.
Pacific Northwest Drupal Summit
This conference is geared toward developers and advanced Drupalers. In 2011, Portland hosted the event; I assisted with the organization, mainly in the area of design.
DrupalCons
My first DrupalCon was a relatively short time ago - Chicago 2011. I got addicted and have attended each of the US ones since as well as DrupalCon Prague 2013 and Barcelona 2015.
Portland, Oregon frontend meetups
In 2011 I started monthly meetups for the local Drupal frontend developers. We discuss topics ranging from designing for the web to Drupal-specific theme implementation to the latest industry tools and techniques.
Other Drupal meetups
Portland has a thriving Drupal community, partially represented in 3+ meetups per month. I have attended them since 2009, and have presented several times, mostly on frontend or site-building topics.
